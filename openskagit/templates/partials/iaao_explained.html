1

Market Grouping

Skagit County isn’t one housing market — it’s several. Homes in Anacortes behave differently than homes in Sedro-Woolley or Concrete. We segment sales into market groups so each area is modeled on its own dynamics.

Why it matters: location-based modeling produces far more accurate and fair valuations.

2

Data Filtering & Cleaning

We remove non-market sales, outliers, and unusual properties that distort the regression. This includes trimming the top/bottom percentiles for price and home size, and excluding luxury properties that follow different pricing patterns.

Why it matters: clean data → stable coefficients → low COD.

3

Feature Engineering

We convert raw property data into statistically meaningful predictors. This includes log-transforming living area, lot size, and age; building time trends; and creating interaction terms to capture how features behave together.

Why it matters: lets the model capture diminishing returns and nonlinear relationships.

4

Regression Modeling

We fit a robust OLS regression for each market group and value tier using thousands of sales. This identifies how much each property feature contributes to price. Predictions are back-transformed using exponential smearing to avoid bias.

Why it matters: the model “learns” real-world pricing behavior directly from the market.

5

Validation & Fairness Checks

We run industry-standard diagnostics including the Coefficient of Dispersion (COD), Price-Related Differential (PRD), and median ratio studies. Models must meet IAAO compliance ranges to be accepted.

Why it matters: ensures fairness across price ranges and neighborhoods.
