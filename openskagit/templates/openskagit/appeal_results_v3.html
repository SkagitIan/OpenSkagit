What is COD?

The Coefficient of Dispersion (COD) measures how consistent property assessments are across similar properties.

What it measures:

"Horizontal equity" – the consistency of appraisals for similar properties, regardless of their value.

Ideal range (IAAO):

Between 5% and 15% is considered acceptable.

Lower COD (Better)
More consistent valuations across similar properties
Higher COD (Worse)
Greater variation, less uniform assessments

What is PRD?

The Price-Related Differential (PRD) tests for vertical assessment inequity in mass appraisals.

What it measures:

Whether high-value and low-value properties are assessed at the same ratio to market value.

How it works:

PRD = Mean sales ratio ÷ Weighted mean sales ratio

PRD = 1.00 (Perfect)
No assessment bias between high and low valued properties
PRD > 1.03 (Regressive)
High-value parcels are relatively under-appraised
PRD < 0.98 (Progressive)
High-value parcels are relatively over-appraised

What is Sales Ratio?

This ratio compares assessed values to actual market values (sale prices).

Ideal Target:

A ratio of 1.0 or 100% where assessed value equals market value.

IAAO Acceptable Range:

Between 0.90 and 1.10 (90% to 110% of market value).

Purpose:

Ensures properties are assessed consistently and fairly relative to their market value.

Above 110%
Properties may be over-assessed relative to market
Below 90%
Properties may be under-assessed relative to market
